What We Believe

Our doctrinal statement is rooted in God’s Word. Lancaster Baptist Church is an independent Baptist church committed to preaching the gospel, discipling believers, and glorifying Christ in all we do.

The Scriptures

We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible, and preserved Word of God. It is our final authority for faith and practice, profitable for doctrine, reproof, correction, and instruction in righteousness.

The Godhead

We believe in one God who eternally exists in three persons—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. These three are coequal, coeternal, and consubstantial.

The Lord Jesus Christ

We believe Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, virgin born, fully God and fully man. He lived a sinless life, died on the cross as our substitutionary sacrifice, rose bodily from the grave, and is coming again.

The Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit convicts of sin, regenerates believers, indwells the church, and empowers Christians for service and holy living. Believers are sealed by the Spirit until the day of redemption.

The Creation and Fall of Man

We believe God created mankind in His own image. Through Adam’s sin, the human race fell and inherited a sin nature. All men are sinners by birth and by choice, separated from God and in need of salvation.

Salvation

We believe salvation is by grace through faith in Jesus Christ alone. The shed blood of Christ provides complete atonement for sin. Repentant sinners who trust Christ are justified, forgiven, and eternally secure.

The Church

We believe the church is a local assembly of baptized believers united for worship, fellowship, prayer, evangelism, and the observance of the ordinances—baptism by immersion and the Lord’s Supper.

Christian Living

We believe Christians are called to live holy lives, separated unto God, and engaged in personal soulwinning, discipleship, and service through the local church.

The Blessed Hope

We believe in the imminent, pretribulational return of Jesus Christ for His church, followed by the tribulation, His millennial reign, the Great White Throne judgment, and the eternal state.
